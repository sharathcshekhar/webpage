My current research focus is on providing flexible and personalized storage on mobile systems. We are working on a system that enables users to personalize how the data is uploaded to the cloud and where their data is stored in the cloud. At the same time, we let the developers develop customized data management solutions. The goal is to let the end users choose from a set of available data management solutions which can be easily installed on any phones.
This system is built on Android and is a work in progress partly based on our previously published workshop paper, BlueMountain. I am currently working with Taeyeon Ki, Dr. Steve Ko and Dr. Karthik Dantu on this project.

BlueMountain

In this paper, we explore the design space of an app instrumentation tool that automatically integrates cloud storage services for Android apps. Our goal is to allow developers to treat all storage operations as local operations, and automatically enable cloud features customized for individual needs of users and developers.
We discuss various scenarios that can benefit from such an automated tool, challenges associated with the development of it, and our ideas to address these challenges. We are extending this idea of providing customizable storage solutions in our current research.

Enabling Automated, Rich, and Versatile Data Management for Android Apps with BlueMountain
Sharath Chandrashekhara, Kyle Marcus, Rakesh G. M. Subramanya, Hrishikesh S. Karve, Karthik Dantu, Steven Y. Ko
Proceedings of the 7th USENIX Workshop on Hot Topics in Storage and File Systems (HotStorage), 2015

Cider

Cider is a block level storage system which allows variable redundancy at the granularity of a single block. I worked on this project with Dr. Vipin Choudhary. The system was initially developed for disk-based systems on Linux.
The design and method we used in the system were patented (Patent pending). We are working on an extension to this work to optimize Cider for SSD based archival storage. We have developed a prototype of an SSD simulator (developed by Mahesh Vamshi) which can simulate a system based on Cider's technology.

System and method for fault-tolerant block data storage
Inventors: Sharath Chandrashekhara, Madhusudhan Ramesh Kumar, and Vipin Choudhary
Application status: Patent filed in April 2015, published on October 2015 (Patent pending).

Pixelsior

Pixelsior is a photo management framework which can manage the photos on a device and can transparently manage content adaptation and image manipulation. Pixelsior also assists the development of image manipulation apps by providing a pluggable interface for image filters.
This is a work in progress, led by Kyungho Jeon. This is based on our initial work which was published in HotStorage, which presents our study of the requirements of a photo management system and a preliminary design.

Pixelsior: Photo Management as a Platform Service for Mobile Apps
Kyungho Jeon, Sharath Chandrashekhara, Karthik Dantu, Steven Y. Ko
Proceedings of the 8th USENIX Workshop on Hot Topics in Storage and File Systems (HotStorage), 2016
