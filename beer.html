When I am not doing my research and out of beer, I am found brewing my own. I love beer and I have camouflaged my beer addiction as a hobby through homebrewing. I am nano scale brewer, brewing beers in batches of 1-2 gallons. I mostly use malt extracts and use partial mashing whenever required. Although every beer I have brewed of late, I had to use partial mashing. I use dry yeast for all my beers and they turn out to be great. I don't pay too much attention to adhere to the strict style guidelines as long as I get great tasting beer. That explains why I decided to brew an Orange Hef! If you live in the Buffalo area and want to get in touch to start brewing, I will be glad to help!

Blueberry Wheat

Tons of blueberry flavour in a American style fruit wheat beer. I used frozen blueberries and the beer turned out to be deep pink in color. By splitting the blueberries between secondary and boil, it has the subtle flavor or blueberry and the wine like tartness.

KBS Clone

This started as a clone of the famous KBS beer. I didn't really have all the made some ingrdients with me. So I made some substitutions to create a similar, but different Russian Imperial Stout. This one is now sitting in secondary and I am expecting an ABV of around 10%. When transeffering to secondary, I bottled the extra beer after my secondary was full. Like the original KBS, it has the deep coffee and chocolate flavours, but the body is much thinner (may be because of dry yeast). If I have to do this again, I will add a bit more oats and some more row-2 and mash it at much lower temperatures.

Vanilla Latte Stout

Here is a coffee milk stout with vanilla additions. It is a delight for coffee lovers. A word of caution before brewing any milk stouts: Most of th brewing softwares treats lactose as a fermentable while estimating the FG and final ABV. To get the desired ABV, you have to add more base malt. I did the same mistake, and the beer was watered down. If I have to brew it again, I will try to hit OG of 1.060, which will still keep the ABV aroung 5.

Honey Ginger Ale

Summer ale brewed with fresh ginger and honey malt. This makes a great summer ale. Intense flavors from ginger and finishes with honey flavor. I used both honey as a late addition and honey malt to get the honey flavors. The recomemeded wayt to add honey is to add it in the secondary after a complex pasteurization. May be I will try that someday! I used Muntons dry yeast for this and the beer turned out to be very cloudy. I wish I had chosen a Safale-04 or 05 instead.

Honey Orange Wheat Ale

Here is an American wheat beer with brewed with lots of orange and honey. Another very drinkable summer ale. I added sweet orange peel to boil, orange zest and the fruit to the secondary. The result was a pleasant citrusy orange beer. Altough I brewed this as an extract beer, I did manage to steep the honey malt at 155F. If I had to do this again, I will partial mash the honey malt with a base malt to get more honey charecter.
