Currently (as of fall '16), I am not assigned any teaching responsibilities and I work as a Research Assistant under The SUNY Research foundation. In the past, I have been a teaching assistant for grad level courses Operating Systems (CSE-421/521) and Distributed Systems (CSE-486/586).

Introduction to Operating Systems

CSE 421/521 is a grad level course on operating systems offered by Dr. Tevfik Kosar. Class projects are based on developing kernel modules for Pintos. I have been a TA for this course during the fall semesters of 2014 and 2015.

Distributed Systems

CSE 486/586 is a grad level course on distributed systems offered by Dr. Steve Ko. Class projects are based on developing distributed system modules on the Android platform. I have been a TA in this course during the spring semesters of 2015 and 2016.

Advanced Computer Systems

CSE 622 is an advanced level grad course on systems offered by Dr. Steve Ko. I have given guest lectures on the topics of storage and mobile systems for this course.

  • Storage in Mobile World (fall '16).
  • Cloud Storage and Mobile Applications (fall '15).
